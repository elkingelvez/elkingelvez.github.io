About Me

I am an Associate Professor and researcher in the Faculty of Basic and Biomedical Sciences at Universidad Simón Bolívar in San José de Cúcuta - Colombia. I am an Associate Researcher at Colombia's Ministry of Science, Technology, and Innovation. I am also a Scientific Modeling and Business Innovation Research Group (GIMCINE) member. My research interests revolve around applied mathematical modeling and mathematics education. My academic background is as follows:

  • Since 2024: Researcher and Professor at Universidad Simón Bolívar (Cúcuta, Colombia).
  • 2020-2023: PhD in Applied Mathematical Modeling in the LITRP (Laboratory for Technological Research in Pattern Recognition) at Universidad Católica del Maule (Chile).
  • 2013-2019: Coordinator and Professor in the Exact Sciences Department at Universidad Simón Bolívar (Cúcuta, Colombia).
  • 2013-2015: Master in Mathematics Education at Universidad Nacional Experimental del Táchira (San Cristobal - Venezuela).
  • 2006-2011: Degree in Mathematics and Computing at Universidad Francisco de Paula Santander (Cúcuta, Colombia).

Research

My research interests focus on mathematical modeling, pattern recognition, and mathematics education. I am dedicated to exploring how mathematical models can be used to understand and predict complex systems. Also, I focus on leveraging neural networks and other advanced techniques to improve data analysis and decision-making processes. Additionally, I am passionate about enhancing the teaching and learning of mathematics, focusing on methodologies that make mathematical concepts more accessible and engaging. Below is a list of the projects in which I have participated.

  • 2022-2025: Mathematical Modeling on Parallel and Distributed Computing based on Extreme Learning Machine using Large-Scale Databases I am the principal investigator of this project, which aims to develop a training method using OS-ELM (Online Sequential Extreme Learning Machine) neural networks for large-scale database classification problems. The objective is to achieve a runtime performance superior to current state-of-the-art methods by employing a mathematical model based on a distributed system. This approach is designed to enhance efficiency and scalability in processing extensive datasets. The project is funded by the ANID (National Agency for Research and Developmen) Chile / Scholarship Program / BECAS DOCTORADO NACIONAL / 2020–21201000.
